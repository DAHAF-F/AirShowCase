# AirShowCase
What it is:
A gesture-controlled product reveal experience built for brand launch events, expo booths, and retail window displays. A visitor opens their hand in front of any laptop or kiosk webcam, and a glowing "hologram" of the product floats above their palm — rotating with wrist movement, scaling with hand spread, and locking a colour variant when they pinch their fingers together. No headset, no controller, no app download. It runs entirely in the browser.
Why it works as a pitch:

The hero of the page is the live demo — not a screenshot or mockup, the actual interactive experience a client would see at their event.
It's positioned for one clear buyer: brands running product launches, plus expo/booth and retail-window use cases as natural extensions.
It ends in a lead-capture form, so the "wow" moment has a next step.

What's under the hood (in plain terms):

A webcam reads hand position in real time (using a tracking library called MediaPipe).
That hand data drives a custom-drawn "product" shape — its position, angle, size, and colour.
Visual polish (particle sparks, glow, ripple effects) makes the interaction feel premium rather than like a tech demo.

Where it's at right now:

Fully built and functional as a live demo. The contact form doesn't send anywhere yet — that's the next step before sending this out to real clients.
